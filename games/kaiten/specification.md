# Specification (Kaiten Party!)

- This is a card game. It can be played with 2 to 8 players
- There is a fixed pool of 181 cards, but only a chosen subset is used in each game (see Menu Selection below)
- Each game consists of 3 rounds. At the beginning of each round each player gets a certain amount of cards dealt from a shuffled draw deck:
    - 2–3 Players: 10 cards each
    - 4–5 Players: 9 cards each
    - 6–7 Players: 8 cards each
    - 8 Players: 7 cards each
    - The remaining cards remain on the draw pile
- Before each round, a number of dessert cards are shuffled into the main deck:
    - 2–5 Players: Round 1: 5 cards, Round 2: 3 cards, Round 3: 2 cards
    - 6–8 Players: Round 1: 7 cards, Round 2: 5 cards, Round 3: 3 cards
- In each turn every player simultaneously selects one of their cards that they would like to keep. If every player has selected one card the selected cards will be revealed and openly put on the players stack. The remaining cards from each player are handed to their next neighbor so in the end each player has a new set of cards.
- Each round consists of as many turns as there are cards because each player keeps 1 card per turn. A round is done when all players have 0 hand cards left.
- After each round points for each player are calculated. Each card or group of cards may yield a certain amount of points. It's also possible to lose points.
- All cards except dessert cards are returned to the draw pile. Dessert cards remain in front of each player across all rounds and are only scored at the end of the game.
- Draw pile is reshuffled (with new dessert cards added) and a new round is started (unless we are already in the 3rd round)
- After the 3rd round the game ends. After each game the total amount of points per player are calculated. First the points of each round are added together. Dessert cards are collected over all rounds; their points are calculated at the end and added to the total score.
- Tie-breaker: In case of equal total points, the player with the most dessert cards wins.

## Menu Selection

- Before each game, players agree on which cards to use. The deck is always assembled from: Nigiri (always included) + 1 type of rolls + 3 types of appetizers + 2 types of specials + 1 type of desserts.
- Predefined menus are provided (see below) but players may also create their own combination.
- Restrictions: Menu and Special Order may not be used in a 7–8 player game. Spoon and Edamame may not be used in a 2 player game.

## Card types

### Nigiri
- Number of cards: 12 total (mix of Egg, Salmon, Squid)
- Type: Nigiri
- Points: Egg Nigiri = 1 pt. Salmon Nigiri = 2 pts. Squid Nigiri = 3 pts. A Nigiri placed on top of a Wasabi card is worth triple points (Egg = 3, Salmon = 6, Squid = 9).
- Special: If a player has an unoccupied Wasabi card when playing a Nigiri, the Nigiri must be placed on top of it.

### Maki Roll
- Number of cards: 12 (cards show 1, 2 or 3 maki icons)
- Type: Sushi Roll
- Points: End of round — player with most maki icons scores 6 pts, player with second most scores 3 pts. In a 6–8 player game: most = 6, second = 4, third = 2. Ties all receive the full points for that place.
- Special: A player must have at least 1 maki roll card to score any maki points.

### Temaki
- Number of cards: 12
- Type: Sushi Roll
- Points: End of round — player with most Temaki cards scores 4 pts. Player with fewest (including 0) loses 4 pts. Ties all receive or lose the full points.
- Special: In a 2 player game no one loses points for fewest Temaki.

### Uramaki
- Number of cards: 12 (cards show varying numbers of icons)
- Type: Sushi Roll
- Points: Mid-round scoring — first player to accumulate 10 or more Uramaki icons immediately scores 8 pts and discards their Uramaki cards. Second to reach 10 scores 5 pts, third scores 2 pts. At end of round, the player with the most remaining Uramaki icons scores the next unclaimed place (8/5/2/0). Ties both receive full points; next place is skipped.
- Special: Scoring and discarding happens immediately mid-round when the threshold is reached.

### Tempura
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: Set of 2 yields 5 points. Multiple sets yield multiple points. Single cards yield 0 points.
- Special: -

### Sashimi
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: Set of 3 yields 10 points. Multiple sets yield multiple points. Fewer than 3 yield 0 points.
- Special: -

### Dumpling
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: 1 card -> 1 point. 2 -> 3. 3 -> 6. 4 -> 10. 5+ -> 15.
- Special: -

### Eel
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: 1 Eel = -3 pts. 2 or more Eel = 7 pts.
- Special: -

### Tofu
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: 1 Tofu = 2 pts. 2 Tofu = 6 pts. 3 or more Tofu = 0 pts.
- Special: -

### Onigiri
- Number of cards: 8 (4 shapes: circle, triangle, square, rectangle — 2 of each)
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: Score based on number of unique shapes collected: 1 unique = 1 pt, 2 unique = 4 pts, 3 unique = 9 pts, 4 unique = 16 pts. Multiple complete sets may be scored.
- Special: Only the number of distinct shapes matters, not total card count.

### Edamame
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: Each Edamame card is worth 1 point per opponent who also has any Edamame cards. Maximum 4 points per card. Worth 0 if no opponent played any Edamame.
- Special: Only use in 3–8 player games.

### Miso Soup
- Number of cards: 8
- Type: Appetizer
- Points: Worth 3 pts at end of round — but only if it was the sole Miso Soup played in its turn. If 2 or more Miso Soups are played on the same turn, all of them are immediately discarded and score nothing.
- Special: Multiple Miso Soups may be scored in a single round (from different turns).

### Chopsticks
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: 0 pts (unused Chopsticks at end of round = 0).
- Special: Bonus action card. On a future turn, call out "chopsticks!" when revealing cards, then play a second card from your hand. Place the Chopsticks card back into your hand to be passed on. Only 1 bonus action card may be used per turn.

### Spoon
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: 0 pts (unused Spoon at end of round = 0).
- Special: Bonus action card. On a future turn, call out "spoon!" when revealing cards, then name any card type. The first player to your left who has it in hand gives it to you; you play it immediately. Give the Spoon to that player. If no one has the card the Spoon is discarded. Only use in 3–8 player games.

### Wasabi
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: 0 pts if no Nigiri is placed on it. Triples the value of the next Nigiri placed on top of it.
- Special: The next Nigiri a player plays must be placed on top of an unoccupied Wasabi. Each Wasabi holds exactly 1 Nigiri. Each nigiri placed on top of a wasabi gets triple points (so )

### Soy Sauce
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: End of round — worth 4 pts if the player has the most (or tied for most) different card background colors among all their played cards this round.
- Special: The Soy Sauce card's own color counts. Multiple Soy Sauce cards may each score in a round.

### Tea
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: End of round — worth 1 pt per card in the player's largest single-color group of played cards this round.
- Special: Multiple Tea cards may each score in a round.

### Menu
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: 0 pts (discarded after use).
- Special: When played, draw 4 cards from the unused deck, choose 1 to play immediately (not another Menu), return the other 3 to the deck and shuffle. Only use in 2–6 player games.

### Special Order
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: Scores as whatever card it is copying.
- Special: Copies any other card already played in front of you this round. Point the card at the target when placing it face-down. If no other card has been played, it is discarded. Only use in 2–6 player games.

### Takeout Box
- Number of cards: 3
- Type: Special
- Points: 0 pts (discarded after use). All cards flipped by it are each worth 2 pts at end of round.
- Special: When played, you may flip any number of your previously played cards face-down. Those cards lose their original identity and are each worth 2 pts instead.

### Pudding
- Number of cards: 15
- Type: Dessert
- Points: Scored at end of game — player with most Pudding cards scores 6 pts. Player with fewest (including 0) loses 6 pts. Ties all receive or lose the full points.
- Special: In a 2 player game no one loses points for fewest Pudding. Cards are kept in front of the player across all 3 rounds.

### Green Tea Ice Cream
- Number of cards: 15
- Type: Dessert
- Points: Scored at end of game — sets of 4 Green Tea Ice Cream cards score 12 pts each. 1, 2, or 3 cards score 0. Multiple sets may be scored.
- Special: Cards are kept in front of the player across all 3 rounds.

### Fruit
- Number of cards: 15 (cards show Watermelon, Orange, and/or Pineapple icons)
- Type: Dessert
- Points: Scored at end of game — each of the 3 fruit types (Watermelon, Orange, Pineapple) is scored independently based on the number of icons of that type: 0 icons = -2 pts, 1 = 0, 2 = 1, 3 = 3, 4 = 6, 5+ = 10.
- Special: Cards are kept in front of the player across all 3 rounds. A player with 0 fruit cards loses 2 pts for each of the 3 types.
